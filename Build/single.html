The 8-bit Nebula Surprise

Written by Colin Devroe · Jan 3, 2014

star star star

The Wide-field Infrared Survey Explorer (WISE) is being used to image asteroids, the dimmest stars, and the coldest objects in our universe.

In the above image it did its job catching more than one asteroid in flight around our solar system. As a bit of a surprise it also managed to capture a cool 8-bit looking image of the Helix nebula which is — surprise, surprise — no where near our solar system. It is, however, in our galaxy at a distance of merely 700 light-years away from Earth. It is our closest neighboring nebula.
